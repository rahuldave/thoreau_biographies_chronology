# Franklin Benjamin Sanborn Chronology

Source keys are defined in `the_book_that_changed_america_chronology.md`.
This chronology keeps Sanborn's long postwar afterlife after Thoreau's death,
even though Fuller returns to Thoreau in chapter 24 after chapter 23 follows
Sanborn beyond 1862.

## Chronology

- **December 15, 1831 - Sanborn is born in Hampton Falls, New Hampshire.** Check: web-confirmed. Sources: W-Sanborn.
  - Summary: Sanborn was born in Hampton Falls, New Hampshire, and came of age before the slavery crisis reached its final phase. Fuller later treats him as the young organizer who links Concord reform culture to Brown and Darwin.
  - Web context: Wikipedia, Franklin Benjamin Sanborn.

  - Summary: Sanborn opened a private school in Concord and quickly entered the town's literary and reform circles. The school connected him to Thoreau, Louisa May Alcott, Emerson, and Bronson Alcott.
  - Book anchor: Chapter 5: Making a Stir (text book.md:451-500; notes book.md:2015-2066).
  - Web context: Wikipedia, Franklin Benjamin Sanborn.

  - Book anchor: Chapter 5: Making a Stir (text book.md:451-500; notes book.md:2015-2066).
  - Web context: Wikipedia, Franklin Benjamin Sanborn; Wikipedia, Kansas-Nebraska Act; Wikipedia, Bleeding Kansas.

## Arc in Fuller

Sanborn turns Origin into a Concord event because his dinner table connects
Darwin's book, Brown's martyrdom, radical abolition, and Transcendental Concord.
His chronology is the clearest reason chapter order cannot be treated as event
order: Fuller's Sanborn story runs far beyond Thoreau's death, then the book
returns to Thoreau's 1860-1862 final work.
